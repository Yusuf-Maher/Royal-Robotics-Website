Our Mission
Bishop Reding Catholic Secondary School is excited to participate in the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) to strengthen students’ passion for technology and innovation. We offer a dynamic Tech Hub , which allows students to explore coding, programming, and hands-on projects, enhancing both technical skills and creativity.

As a school of opportunity and inclusivity, in addition to clubs, we offer specialized classes in computer science and engineering , along with Advanced Placement (AP) programs and Specialist High School Major(SHSM) These offerings give students a strong foundation in technical knowledge. By participating in FRC, students can apply their skills in a competitive environment, fostering teamwork, and problem-solving abilities while dealing with complex challenges.

Our Robot The FIRST Robotics Competition aligns perfectly with our school's vision. It allows our students to apply their coding and tech skills in a real-world, competitive setting. We challenge them to collaborate, innovate, and engineer solutions to complex problems, having not only their technical acumen but also their teamwork and problem-solving abilities. As a group we come together to build and program our robot. Here are just a few out of the many components in ours.
Fabrication In our workspace we heavily used our 2 X-carve CNC to cut .090” thick 6061 aluminum, and ⅜” poly carbonate. We also used our sienci long mill mk1 to aid in drilling precise hole in 1”x1”, and 2”x1” extrusions. Many of our more geometrically complex parts were sent out to our gracious sponsor “Sable Metal” to be laser cut from 6061 aluminum and we thank them for all the support they have given us!
Drivetrain Chassis
MK4i Swerve Modules with L2 gear ratios driven by 2 NEO V1.1 with Spark Max controllers with a max speed of 4.9 m/s.
YAGSL used for swerve drive software.
32 in long by 27.5 in wide.
2056 style 3D Printed swerve covers (Not Pictured in Render).
0.090” thick laser cut cut aluminum base plate.
Power Distribution Hub Riser for Wire Routing.
Intake For our design we required an under-bumper intake that would feed the note directly into the shooter. We need it to be quick, robust, and easy to service all while being gentle with the Note.
NEO V.1 drives the intake with a 14 to 45 gear reduction.
2” Thrifty Bot Squish wheels paired with ¼” Round Polyurethane belts are used to intake the game piece for their high friction, coefficient, and compatibility reducing game piece damage. 
Intake has ½” of compression on the Note.
Teflon Fabric Tape used to reduce friction on intake ramp surface.
Attached to Front Chassis Rail using “T-Nut” Style slots in the ⅜” Polycarbonate intake side plates.
